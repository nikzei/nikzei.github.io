Nikki Zeichner

I am a product manager, design researcher and ux designer for civic tech projects. I'm a New Yorker living in San Francisco.

In November, I completed a fellowship with Code for America where I worked as part of a three person team to build open source citizen engagement tools for the City of Vallejo, California. Currently, I research for Code for America's health team, which is focused on improving access to food stamps in California. I came to Code for America after completing an M.S. in Integrated Digital Media at NYU's Engineering School where I was a Women in Technology fellow and a Project Management Fellow with the GovLab@NYU.

Before returning to graduate school, I practiced as a criminal defense attorney in New York. I transitioned toward technology to explore ways of improving governmental systems that I found so difficult to work within. Having years of experience working on nonprofit campaigns and advocating for vulnerable clients has certainly taught me invaluable skills in humility, empathy, communication, risk-management, and strategy. I recently moderated a panel, Building Digital Tools for Legal Service Providers, at the Code for America Summit because I am passionate about exploring ways that digital technologies can help low income individuals access justice. I'm always happy to chat about topics related to this with anyone who is interested.

I like cooking, biking, conversations, multi-media storytelling, museums and the beach. I also practice Ashtanga yoga. If you want me to help you with yoga postures, come by Mission Ashtanga in Potrero Hill on a Sunday morning when I assist during Mysore practice.
